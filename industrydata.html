Industry Data

Comparison of group job profiles with Burning Glass industry data

Ideal jobs

The ideal Job titles of each group member are listed in the table below. In order to compare these to the Burning Glass industry data, we had to match them with existing job profiles that might differ slightly to the selected ones. The following adjustments have been made for comparison:

  • Data Scientist → Senior Data Engineer
  • Head of Digital Marketing & e-Commerce → Customer Engagement Manager
  • Divisional IT Manager → IT Operations Manager
  • ICT Service Desk Officer → ICT Support Officer

Every job profile is divided into five general skills and five IT skills. Those skills are ranked according to the Top 25 General/IT Skills Burning Glass industry data. The lowest score achievable is 15 and means that the ideal job profile demands the Top 5 skills in a category. The highest score is 115 and means that the ideal job profile demands the 5 lowest scored skills in a category.

Matching the ideal job profiles of all group members with the Top IT Jobs reveals a rather low ranking for all positions.

IT Skills

The industry data counts job postings as senior management positions, and as such, the number of postings is expected to be much lower than other jobs like Java Developers, Test Analysts or Software Engineers. Despite this, all group members have decided to pursue a career in their chosen job category and have not changed opinions after seeing Burning Glass industry data.

The average value for the category “IT Skills” in all job profiles is 48, meaning that the IT skills demanded for each job are also among the top skills demanded in the industry. Again, the Divisional IT Manager skills set is the most demanded with a score of 28.

The three highest ranked “IT Skills” that cannot be found in any of the group’s ideal jobs are SAP, Building Relationships and Graphic Design.
