Group Reflections

Looking back at how far we have come

The Gracehoppers Group Reflection

The Good

Overall as a team we function quite well everyone has a great work ethic which has allowed us to get through a large volume of work in the time we have available in our busy lives. Having quality project management tools available to us such as Jira and miro has been a great advantage as everyone knew what everyone else was doing which reduced a lot of unnecessary communication overheads and allowed us to focus on our tasks. Our weekly meetings allowed us time to discuss at length any difficulties that we were facing either individually or as a team.

The Bad

We had one team member that did not step up and fulfil their responsibilities which reduced our effective overall capacity by twenty percent and increased the workload on the rest of the team significantly. Everyone had a lot going on in their lives and had to make efforts to involve themselves with this project. This is one of the parts of groupwork that can be extremely difficult what do you do with members that are not making efforts. It was decided that we could not continue with a team member that was not performing to even a minimum standard.

The Unexpected

In our group we have a diverse array of skills and knowledge, during this project individual team members were able to step up and take on parts of the project that they could showcase their abilities in. The effectiveness of this was felt by the entire team as we were able to play to our strengths instead of being forced to work on our weaknesses. This is a primary example of why it can be a great advantage working in a group, everyone brings something different to the table which allows a higher quality of work to be produced in the timeframe available.

The Lesson

Key take away from this are mainly in the area of project planning and development. As a team we have developed a large body of work on the plans for the Smatra application and it has been quite an achievement. It has become its own entity with a vast amount of information available of what it is what it does and how it will be constructed. The what if game of what it would become with the extra 10 were spent in developing this project is a very interesting game to play. If this project was fully funded with our current team there is no doubt of its success.

Michael Robertson

The Good

We started off with the same team that already had delivered a fantastic job in the previous assignment. Again, this has been an amazing group experience! I have worked with teams in the past that did not work well together but the Gracehoppers surprised me over and over again with attending meetings, contributing ideas and getting the assignment completed.

The Bad

We spent a lot of time in the planning phase again. This time, we focused more on brainstorming and collecting thoughts and ideas before going ahead with the project. This is not a bad practice by any means, especially considering the scope of activities but with the tight time schedule and other commitments (full-time work, other University subjects and family) we should have pushed a little harder to get the project started.

The Unexpected

It was surprising to see one of the group members drop off the team completely, right at the start of the project. Having less members in the group, meant more work for the rest of us. This was unexpected and very unfortunate for the team.

The Lesson

This project has taught me that studying at University and working together in groups can be a lot of fun. In my professional work life, this has not always been the case. Perhaps it is the educational environment that is less competitive and more relaxing. After all, we have decided to study and do the work required without being forced. This is different at work, where I keep on working with colleagues who do not seem to be happy where they are and with what they are doing.
