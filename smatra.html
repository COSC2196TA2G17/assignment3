Topic

The World Economic Forum lists five major challenges for big cities of the future on their website. Our project idea addresses three out of the five while it will also have an impact on the remaining two eventually. The challenges identified by the World Economic Forum are Environmental Threats, Resources, Inequality, Technology and Governance. Our project idea is a value proposition that can help big cities transform their transport networks for a sustainable future. (Chee, 2019) The idea is to develop a mobile app, combining a loyalty program (like FlyBuys or Velocity) with a mobility planning tool, incentivising the use of public modes of transportation, or the use of toll roads outside of peak hour traffic. The app usage by commuters is seeking to ease traffic congestion around major arteries of Australian capital cities. Therefore, the App requires an integrated approach; tapping into third party applications like Toll Road, Public Transport or Parking Apps to facilitate commuters into desired forms of transportation.

The five challenges the World Economic Forum has identified are all based on one underlying trend which is the urbanisation in modern industrialised countries. Urbanisation is defined as an increase in population in cities and towns rather than rural areas. Infrastructure Australia, an independent statutory body for research and advice on infrastructure needs, claims that Australia’s cities are even facing a watershed moment regarding their growth trend and development. Between 2017 and 2046 Australia’s population is projected to grow by 11.8 million people. And up to 75% of this increase in population with occur in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane and Perth. (Infrastructure Australia, 2018) The opportunities for cities are big but so are the risks. Environmental Threats by a growing number of vehicles on the roads for example. Resources will come under scrutiny and electricity grids might reach their max capacity. Lastly, Technology can help cities adapt, however, to master the right applications can be quite challenging. This is where our project idea comes into play. A technological app solution to manage resources more efficiently in order to avoid environmental threats and educate a new generation of commuters.

Motivation

The motivation for this project stems from personal experience but also from seeing the bigger picture in a world that is facing climate change and its implications all around the world. Cities like Melbourne or Sydney are experiencing incredible population growth numbers and therefore an ever increasing amount of vehicles on major arteries. In a group discussion at the start of this assignment we all realised that being stuck in traffic is a situation we are all too familiar with. Furthermore, we were all realising that with the current transportation network and the increasing number of vehicles on our Australian roads, cities would eventually collapse under the demands of commuters with a catastrophic result for the environment and the living conditions in major urban centres. A solution addressing this situation and allowing subsequent generations to thrive is a challenge, but with one of the major IT trends being “ Advanced Networking” on the backbone of the introduction of 5G technology and new IoT standards as well as the rise of smart cities we think our project is able to succeed. (Deloitte Australia, 2019) As private as well as public modes of transportation on macro or micro level become more interconnected, the only thing we have had to add to the equation was the rewards program in order to incentivise smarter transport choices and therefore educate society. In order to target the daily commuter in the most efficient way, the only realistic option was the development of an app to bring our services together and provide a smarter digital touchpoint. In our view, this project has incredible potential and with the right partners could even be realised in the long run. Future employers would be able to recognise the complexity of the project and realise that only with precise planning, open communication and the right IT skill sets combined in our team this endeavour is manageable. In the end, this project might even attract one of the major 5G network companies to assist in order to gain public reputation as a sustainable and innovative market player.
